Borscht



Description

Borscht is a soup, usually made with beets, originating from Central and Eastern Europe and Northern Asia. It is especially popular in the cuisines of Russia, Poland, Lithuania, Romania, Latvia, and Ukraine. Although the beet version is the most well known, it doesn't have to be made with beets. Borscht was a winter soup, often made with sour flavors and topped with a dollop of sour cream. It was usually made by combining meat or bone stock with sautéed or boiled vegetables. Popular ingredients may include cabbage, carrots, onions, potatoes and/or tomatoes. Sometimes it's puréed; sometimes it's not. Sometimes there's meat; sometimes there's not. The name of the ancient tart soup is now more associated with the beet-red soup of today. If you want to recreate that traditional tartness, you can serve with lemon wedges on the side or add more vinegar, to taste.


Ingredients

  • 3 teaspoons extra virgin olive oil or vegetable oil
  • bone-in beef shank with a lot of meat
  • 1 large onion
  • 8 cups beef broth
  • 4 large beets
  • 4 carrots
  • 1 large russet potato
  • 2 cups thinly sliced cabbage
  • 3/4 cup chopped fresh dill
  • 3 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • salt and freshly ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Heat 2 teaspoons oil in a large, thick-bottomed pot on medium high heat.
  2. Brown the beef lightly on one side, then turn over.
  3. Add the onions and cook about 5 minutes.
  4. Add 4 cups broth and simmer until the beef is tender.
  5. Meanwhile, prep and roast the root vegetables. Roast in preheated oven for 15 minutes.
  6. Remove the meat from the pot and chop into bite sized pieces.
  7. Skim off any excess fat from the liquid in the pot.
  8. Return the pot to the stove and add the remaining broth, the carrots, beets, and potato.
  9. Add the chopped meat to the pot, the sliced cabbage, and a half cup of the fresh dill.
  10. Bring to a simmer, and cook for another 15 minutes or so, until the cabbage is cooked through.
  11. Add the vinegar and season to taste with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

How to serve this purple portion?

Serve hot with sour cream!
